As We May Think by Vannevar Bush, 1945


My Thoughts

In As We May Think by Vannevar Bush, Bush talks about how scientists should develop inventions that store information and knowledge rather than just to "extend man's physical powers." Near the end, he talks about an invention called the "memex." He wants to come up with a way to store information and access it. The idea that he is coming up with is similar to that of the world wide web. This really made me think about the importance of the world wide web. Before reading this, I didn't think about how data is collected, organized, and stored thru the web.

Long Live the Web by Tim Berners Lee, 2010


My Thoughts

This reading made me realize and think more about the role net neutrality plays in today's society. It also made me think about how much net neutrality can influence consumer behavior. The idea of net neutrality is that ISP (internet service providers) should allow access to all content and applications regardless of the source that content or application. ISP should not be allowed to slow down content from one source and not another. So in a way, all content should be displayed to the user in a way that is unbiased. Before reading that article, I did not think or realize how large of a role net neutrality plays in consumer behavior. For instance, if an ISP makes it easier to access one online store rather than another, that customer would more likely visit the store that is easier to access. Thus it made me realize the importance of net neutrality.
